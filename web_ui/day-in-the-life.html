SE OS · The Reinvention

A Tuesday in the life of a Microsoft SE

Same customer. Same meeting. Two very different days.

Today · Without SE OS
The Assembly Day
Tuesday — customer meeting at 2 PM
8:30 AM
Open laptop. Try to remember which D365 demo env you used for this customer last time.
~15 min lost just orienting
9:00 AM
Hunt for context. Open Outlook, Teams, OneNote, SharePoint, the CRM, the deal site, and your demo OneDrive folder. 7 tabs. Skim emails. Find the relevant deck from 3 weeks ago.
35 min · context lives in 7 places
9:35 AM
Find your demo script. Last time you customized it for this customer's vertical. Where did you save that? OneDrive — no, the desktop — wait, the Teams chat.
20 min hunting through personal storage
9:55 AM
Refresh the demo env. Log into CDX, check if records were reset. They weren't. Manual cleanup. Hope nothing broke.
40 min · environment hygiene is on you
10:35 AM
Tailor for industry. Customer is automotive. Demo data is generic mfg. Manually swap product names, account names, terminology so it doesn't look like a cookie-cutter pitch.
45 min of cosmetic editing
11:20 AM
Confirm meeting details. Re-read the email thread. Try to remember what the customer's pain point was. Pull their org chart.
20 min · re-doing context you had 3 weeks ago
11:40 AM
Lunch (rushed). Eating while reviewing the deck. You haven't talked to a colleague all morning.
12:30 PM
Practice demo run. Discover something broke in the env from a recent platform update. Slack a colleague. Wait for a workaround.
45 min lost to brittle env
1:30 PM
30 min buffer. Should be reviewing the customer's strategic priorities. Mostly just hoping the demo works.
2:00 PM
Customer meeting. You're prepared, but it took 5 hours of front-loaded work and your morning is gone.
Total prep: ~5 hours · for one 60-minute meeting
3:30 PM
Post-meeting follow-up. Will get to it tomorrow. Maybe.
Half your notes will never get logged
Tomorrow · With SE OS
The Customer Day
Tuesday — same customer meeting at 2 PM
8:30 AM
Open SE Command Center. Dashboard shows: 2 PM customer is highlighted. Brief is already drafted. Demo env health is green.
Context is already assembled · Maya prepped overnight
8:35 AM
Review the brief. Felix (Outcome Framer) flagged that the customer's CFO joined the call list — added a "cost-to-serve" angle to the narrative. Priya (Industry SME) added a 30-second framing for automotive supply chain context.
10 min reading · everything is contextualized
8:45 AM
Skim the demo guide. Auto-tailored for the customer's vertical. Account names, products, scenarios already swapped. Five-beat narrative arc clean.
Jonas + Vera handled the customization · 15 min review
9:00 AM
One question to the Digital SE Team. "Has this customer talked to anyone else at MSFT in the last 90 days?" Maya checks, returns thread with names + summary.
5 min · no internal stalking required
9:05 AM
You have 4 hours back. Real choices: deep technical prep, a colleague conversation, working on the *next* customer pipeline, or building a reference architecture you've wanted to ship.
+4 hours · the entire morning is yours
9:30 AM
Choose: shipping a new use case. File a GitHub issue with "automotive warranty + Copilot Studio." Sam + Diego start building. Iris kicks off the eval.
Your time is now compounding for the team
11:00 AM
Coffee with a colleague. Not because you have time to kill — because relationships and cross-team learning is now part of the job, not what gets cut.
1:00 PM
Rowan posts a heads-up. "Demo env health flag: one Power Automate flow timed out in the last regression run — using fallback. Demo is safe."
You knew before the customer did
2:00 PM
Customer meeting. You're prepared, focused, and the demo is solid. You can be present in the conversation because the assembly was done for you.
Total prep: ~30 min · for the same 60-min meeting
3:30 PM
Post-meeting auto-debrief. Open Command Center, dictate 90-second voice memo: "Customer asked about X, said yes to Y, follow-up by Friday." Jonas writes the follow-up email draft. Aria logs the outcome. Maya schedules the follow-up.
5 min · nothing falls through the cracks
