1. What this is

The HKP RMA Operations app is a Model-Driven workbench for warranty/RMA triage engineers at AMETEK Haydon Kerk Pittman. It replaces the spreadsheet+inbox workflow with:

  • A shared mailbox listener (rmarequest@D365DemoTSCE30330346.onmicrosoft.com) that ingests every customer return request
  • AI Builder that extracts 17 structured fields from the email body (part #, customer name, failure mode, etc.)
  • A queue UI in Dataverse where the operator reviews the AI's extraction and converts it to an RMA Claim
  • A Pizza Tracker on every claim form showing exactly which stage it's in and how long it's been there
  • One-click resolve commands (Credit / Replacement / Repair / Deny) with threshold-based manager approval gating
  • A kanban board for the team lead showing every open claim grouped by status
The headline: from email arrival to closed RMA in <5 minutes — instead of the multi-day cycle the previous spreadsheet workflow allowed.

3. The 5-minute click-through demo

Demo this end-to-end. The whole flow is live and reproducible.

Step 1 — Customer email arrives

1

Send a test RMA email to:

RECIPIENT:
rmarequest@D365DemoTSCE30330346.onmicrosoft.com

Suggested test subject + body:

SUBJECT:
RMA request — WTB-MOTOR-9999 failing on bench test
BODY:
Hi RMA Team, We received iDEA-57 motor part WTB-MOTOR-9999 last week. Three units are showing intermittent stall under load and one failed completely in our QA test rig. PO 88542. Need a replacement plus an RCA on the failure. Quantity: 4 Customer: Andersen Windows Region: Americas Detected during: Production line ramp-up Thanks, QA Engineering, Andersen
The RMA Email Monitor Power Automate flow polls the mailbox every minute, runs the RMA Email Extractor AI Builder prompt, and writes the parsed fields into both a SharePoint backup list AND a Dataverse rma_emaillog record (direction = Inbound, isProcessed = false).

Step 2 — Operator opens the inbox queue

2

In the app, click Email Inbox in the left nav.

The default view "Inbound — Unprocessed" shows every Inbound email with isProcessed = false, newest first. The new test email is at the top with the body preview, sender address, and a blank Linked Claim column.
"This is the morning triage queue. Yesterday's emails are already gone — they're in the Processed view. This is the work for today."

Step 3 — Convert email to RMA Claim

3

Click the email row → form opens with two tabs:

  • General — From, Recipient, Received, Subject, Body Preview, Body (full)
  • Linked Claim — empty (no claim yet)

Click the command bar "Create RMA Claim from Email" (see the Bill TODO for wiring this button — the function is already loaded; the click handler points to HKPCommands.createClaimFromEmail).

A Quick Create dialog opens with Customer Name, Customer Email, Failure Description, and Source Email ID prefilled from the email. The operator picks the Customer Region, Part Number, Quantity, Failure Mode, and (optionally) Assigned Plant — or leaves blank to let the auto-assign flow handle it.
"Notice we didn't retype anything. The AI extracted the body, and we just confirmed the structured fields. Five seconds, not five minutes."

Step 4 — Watch the Pizza Tracker on the new claim

4

The Quick Create saves and (depending on how Bill wires it) the operator navigates to the new RMA Claim form.

At the top of the form: the Pizza Tracker — a 5-step visual showing New → Triage → Investigation → Decision → Closed. The first dot is filled and pulsing blue. Below it: "In New for 0 days · Total age: 0 days".

Below the tracker: 4 tabs

  • General — Customer & Part section · Plant & Status section · Resolution section
  • Notes — Claim Notes subgrid · Timeline (activities, emails, posts)
  • Approvals — Approval Requests · Approval History
  • Emails — Email Log subgrid (the original inbound email is already linked here, plus any outbound replies)
The Pizza Tracker is the heart of the demo. It visualizes the same information a Business Process Flow would, but inline at the top of the form where it's always visible. No "Process" button to click, no hidden flyout.

Step 5 — Resolve the claim

5

Set the Status to Triage, then Investigation, then Decision — watch the Pizza Tracker advance live. (In production the Status changes will come from operator decisions or the stage-tracker flow.)

In Decision stage, click one of the modern command buttons:

CommandBehavior
Resolve — Credit IssuedIf credit ≤ plant threshold → confirms and closes. If credit > threshold → creates an Approval Request and waits.
Resolve — Replacement SentCloses claim with Replacement resolution.
Resolve — Repair CompletedCloses claim with Repair resolution.
Deny ClaimSets resolution to Denied + creates an Approval History entry.
Send Customer EmailOpens email Quick Create prefilled with customer address; logs to Email tab as Outbound.
Request Manager ApprovalCreates an Approval Record under the assigned plant's approvers (currently admin@ placeholder).
On resolve, Pizza Tracker dot 5 (Closed) fills green, status flips, closed-date stamps to now(). Total age shows the elapsed days. The kanban board now shows this card under the Closed column.
"That's the full loop. Email in. Triage. Decide. Close. A complete cycle in well under five minutes — and every action is auditable in Dataverse."

6. Operator daily checklist (post your kanban as a sticky)

  1. Open RMA Operations app → Email Inbox
  2. For each Inbound — Unprocessed row, top to bottom:
    1. Click the row. Read the AI-extracted fields on the Linked Claim tab.
    2. If it's a valid RMA request → Create RMA Claim from Email.
    3. If it's spam / not an RMA → set rma_isprocessed=true manually and leave Linked Claim blank.
  3. Open the new claim. Verify the assigned plant (auto-assigned by part prefix) — override if wrong.
  4. Move Status → Triage. Pizza Tracker advances. Stage Tracker flow stamps rma_stageenteredon.
  5. Investigate. Add Notes. Send customer email if you need more info.
  6. Move Status → Investigation → Decision.
  7. Click the resolve command that matches the outcome. Approval gate triggers if credit > plant threshold.
  8. Claim closes. Next email in the queue.

How to publish a customization change

RUN:
pac auth select --name mfg-gold pac solution publish

Do NOT use the REST PublishXml action. The AppCopilotFeatures managed plugin stalls those calls in this tenant. pac CLI bypasses the queue.

How to add a new plant

  1. Open the Plants table in the app sitemap → New
  2. Set Name, Region, Part Prefixes (comma-separated), Auto-credit Threshold
  3. Add a Plant Approver (rma_plantapprover) record linked to the new plant
  4. Add Routing Rule entries linking part prefixes to the new plant
  5. Done — auto-assign flow uses these immediately

How to test the email pipeline

  1. Send an email to rmarequest@D365DemoTSCE30330346.onmicrosoft.com
  2. Wait up to 1 minute (the flow polls every 60 seconds)
  3. Open the app → Email Inbox → the new row appears at the top of Inbound — Unprocessed
  4. If nothing appears: check Power Automate run history for "RMA Email Monitor"
