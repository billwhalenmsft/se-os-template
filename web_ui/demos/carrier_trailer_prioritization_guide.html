⚡ The 60-Second Brief

Carrier's aftermarket warehouse in Chattanooga, TN (operated by 3PL Kenco) receives ~190 inbound trailers per week — but only has dock space for 20 trailers per day.

That means someone has to decide which 20 trucks get in first, every day, every week.

Today, that "someone" is Sonali Horan — the RC Materials Planning Manager. Every Monday she spends ~6 hours doing this manually in spreadsheets. One person. No automation. Not sustainable.

What we built: A Power Platform app that does it automatically. SAP data flows in → scoring engine ranks every trailer by OTIF + revenue → Canvas App lets Sonali review → one button sends the schedule to Kenco.

📖 Key Terminology You Need to Know
OTIF — On Time In Full
Carrier's #1 supply chain metric. Did the customer's order arrive complete and on time? A trailer with 47 parts on backorder means 47 customers are waiting. Getting that trailer in first directly improves OTIF.
Backlog
Customer orders that can't ship because the warehouse doesn't have the inventory. Backlog count and revenue value are the primary scoring inputs for trailer prioritization.
Composite Score
Every trailer gets a score: (OTIF weight × OTIF score) + (Revenue weight × Revenue score). Default: 50% OTIF, 40% revenue, 10% detention. Fully configurable in Dataverse.
Dock 1 / Dock 2 / Overflow
Three receiving areas at Kenco's Chattanooga facility.
• Dock 1: 5 trailers/day (highest priority)
• Dock 2: 15 trailers/day (bulk)
• Overflow: 3 trailers/day (low backlog)
TSP — Transportation Service Provider
The ~30 trucking companies moving Carrier's trailers. They email the Kenco inbound scheduler to reschedule when mechanical issues or weather cause delays.
Kenco IB Scheduler
The person at 3PL Kenco who books actual dock appointments. Sonali sends them the weekly priority schedule; they coordinate timing with TSPs. This is who receives the auto-generated email.
Detention / Demurrage
Fees Carrier pays when a trailer sits in a yard without being unloaded on time. Adds cost pressure for certain trailers. Phase 2 scoring input — data currently comes via email from logistics.
E2Open
External logistics scheduling platform Carrier uses for some supplier coordination. Not integrated yet — identified as a Phase 2/3 target. Sonali is getting API details from their E2Open contact.
TMS ID
Transport Management System ID — the unique identifier for each trailer (e.g., "TMS-4821"). Used across all data sources to track a specific truck.

🛡️ What To Say When...
"We already have a process."
Sonali told us the current process isn't sustainable for one person and takes 6 hours every Monday. This doesn't replace her judgment — it does the data mining for her so she can focus on strategic work.
"How does this connect to SAP?"
Power Automate has a native SAP connector. Once Samyak configures the credentials, the Monday morning ingestion runs automatically. The 3 non-SAP factories send Excel — that flows in from SharePoint.
"What about our 800 external suppliers?"
Phase 1 handles your 40 full-truckload suppliers — the ones that matter for this use case. The Field Mapping table normalizes their different column names automatically. Phase 3 adds AI Builder for the truly unstructured formats.
"Can we pilot this first?"
Yes — it's already live in a Mfg Gold Template environment. We have a portable solution zip that imports to any Power Platform environment. Samyak can validate the data against your real SAP for one week before going live.
"Who maintains this?"
Very little maintenance required. The scoring weights are in a config table — no code. Adding a new supplier or dock is a row in Dataverse. Power Automate handles the scheduling. Samyak can own this with current Power Platform skills.
"Can we export this to production?"
Yes — we exported a CarrierTrailerPrioritization.zip solution file. Import it to any Power Platform environment with one PAC CLI command. Zero rework.
