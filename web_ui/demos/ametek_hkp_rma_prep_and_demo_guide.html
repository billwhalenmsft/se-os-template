1. Get your head locked in (5 min read)

The use case in one sentence

AMETEK HKP runs warranty / RMA triage out of a shared inbox — engineers manually decide if each request is eligible, what to do (repair / replace / reject), and route it. We built 6 RAPP agents that do the triage in seconds, with a human in the loop on every decision.

Why this customer, why now

  • Existing investment: They already built a `WarrantyandClaimOperations` Dataverse solution (v1.0.0.14) with 29 entities + 10 Power Automate flows. They've put real work in. We add the agent layer on top — we don't replace anything.
  • Quantified pain: "Several days" cycle time. Outcomes vary by who's on shift. They explicitly raised "scaling beyond one person's machine" as the gap.
  • Precedent: AMETEK's SFMS division has another agent build in flight (Use Case 1). HKP is the second beachhead — different division, same playbook.

Three things you must remember on the call

  1. Reuse story is the headline. "We're not replacing your solution — we're extending it." 80% reuse / 20% net-new.
  2. Human in the loop. Every agent recommendation has a confidence + rationale. Engineer always has final say. Never say "automated" — say "agent-assisted."
  3. Get them talking. The agents we built use generic failure-mode keywords. The real value comes from their vocabulary. The call's job is to capture that.

5b. Discovery questions — listen for 7 min (5–12)

Ask 3-4 of these. Listen. Take notes in their words.

  1. Walk me through the last RMA you handled — what did you actually do, click by click?
  2. How long does that whole process take, in minutes? And how much of that is data gathering vs deciding?
  3. If the engineer who handles RMAs is on PTO, what happens?
  4. What are the top 3 or 4 failure modes you see on iDEA motors? What words do customers actually use to describe them?
  5. When do you say no to an RMA? What's the most common reason for rejection?
  6. How does the customer find out where their RMA stands today?
  7. Do you have warranty terms written down somewhere? OEM-specific contract language?
  8. What's an RMA you've gotten in the last month where you weren't sure what to do?
Listening goal: capture 5-10 verbatim phrases. Things like "we usually advance-replace if it's a Tier 1 OEM" or "we always make customers prove the cycle count first." Those phrases go straight into the agent instructions next session.

8. Talking-point cheat sheet

Phrases to use

  • "Agent-assisted" (not "automated")
  • "Confidence + rationale" (every recommendation has both)
  • "Human in the loop" (engineer always reviews)
  • "Calibrated to your vocabulary" (we're flexible)
  • "Reuse what you've built" (their solution is the foundation)
  • "Faster + more consistent" (the two value levers)

Phrases to avoid

  • "Replace your engineers" — never. The pitch is amplification.
  • "AI will decide" — say "AI will recommend, engineer decides."
  • "Production-ready" — it's not yet. Say "demo working, calibration next."
  • "It learns" — without RAG/training pipeline that's a stretch. Say "uses historical context."

If they ask "is this Microsoft tech?"

Yes — the agents run on Azure, the orchestrator is Copilot Studio in your existing tenant, the data lives in your existing Dataverse. We're not bringing a new vendor. We're putting Microsoft's agent stack on top of the Microsoft solution you already have.

If they ask "what about other AMETEK divisions?"

Same playbook. We've got SFMS in flight on a different agent set. The pattern of "extend the existing Dataverse + flows with an agent layer" works for any division that has a similar warranty/claim or proposal/quote workflow.
