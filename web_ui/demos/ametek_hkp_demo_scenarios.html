Scenario 1 — Andersen lead screw failure (in warranty, clear defect)

TIER 1 EXPECTED: APPROVE_REPLACE

Serial: IDEA-57-2025-104872 (in warranty, 18 mo remaining) · Failure mode: lead screw / thread shear (manufacturing defect keyword) · OEM: Andersen Windows OEM · Cost: $4,200 (under $7,500 Tier 1 cap)

Subject:

Lead screw stripped after 6 months — RMA request

Body:

Hi, Customer reports premature lead-screw failure on iDEA 57mm actuator (serial IDEA-57-2025-104872) at 6 months in service. Unit installed in vertical sliding window, normal duty cycle. Photo attached shows clear thread shear. Original PO #PO-AND-2025-0814. Regards, Sarah Andersen Windows
What the agent should do: Extract entities → match Tier 1 (andersenwindows) → look up serial → confirm in warranty → match "lead screw" + "thread shear" defect keywords → recommend approve_replace with confidence ~0.97 (0.92 base + 0.05 Tier 1 boost) → email Sarah with shipping label info → create cr74e_warrantyclaim record → post Teams notification.

Hello, One of our customers is reporting their iDEA 28mm actuator stopped working a month after install. They mentioned the install location got hit with significant water damage during a storm — there was standing water in the cabinet for several days before they found it. They want a replacement under warranty since the unit is under a year old. Serial: IDEA-28-2025-201155 Original PO: PO-ACME-2025-2200 Customer name: Bob Stevens Please advise on next steps. Thanks, Priya Acme Window Distribution
What the agent should do: Extract entities → match Tier 3 (no domain match → distributor default) → look up serial → confirm in warranty → BUT detect "water damage" + "standing water" misuse keywords → reject with confidence 0.95 → email Priya politely declining (warranty exclusion under HKP terms — water damage), offer paid-repair quote → create cr74e_warrantyclaim record (Status: Rejected - Misuse). The fact that it's IN warranty is irrelevant — misuse is rule #1 and overrides.
