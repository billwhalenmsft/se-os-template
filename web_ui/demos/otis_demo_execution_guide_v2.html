Their World Today

Acknowledge pain points without dwelling
~5 min
▼
Say

"Before we dive in, I want to make sure I understand the challenges you're facing today. Based on our conversations, I heard a few things that really resonated..."

🔥 Key Pain Points to Acknowledge:
  • ⏱️ Entrapment response delays — When someone is trapped, every minute matters. Manual dispatch processes slow down your response time when it's most critical.
  • 🔍 Customer context missing — Agents answer the phone without knowing who's calling, what elevators they have, or their service history. Customers have to repeat themselves every time.
  • 📊 No real-time visibility — Supervisors can't see what's happening across the service team. Are SLAs being met? How many entrapments today? You don't know until someone pulls a report.
  • 🧠 Knowledge trapped in people's heads — Your best technicians have decades of experience, but that knowledge isn't captured anywhere. New agents struggle because procedures aren't readily accessible.
  • 📝 Contract details hard to find — When a customer asks "Is this covered?" agents scramble to find the answer. Full vs. Comprehensive vs. Basic — what's included? It shouldn't take 5 minutes to answer.
Say

"First, response time on entrapments. When someone's stuck in an elevator, panic sets in fast. But today, your agents have to manually look up customer info, check the service contract, figure out which technician is closest — all while someone's trapped. Every second counts."

"Second, your agents are flying blind. A call comes in, they don't know who it is. Even if caller ID shows a number, they're asking 'What building are you calling from? What elevator?' The customer already gave that information last week. They're frustrated before the conversation even starts."

"Third, supervisors don't have visibility. How many entrapments happened this week? What's our average response time? Are we meeting our SLA commitments? Right now, answering those questions means waiting for someone to build a spreadsheet."

"And finally, knowledge is locked up. Your veteran technicians know everything — which elevator models have quirks, what the common issues are at specific buildings. But when they retire or when a new agent starts, that knowledge doesn't transfer."

Pause
Wait for acknowledgment. Watch for nodding or verbal confirmation. This builds trust that you understand their world.
Say

"Does that resonate with what you're experiencing? [Let them respond — they may add details or prioritize which pain points matter most to them]"

"Good. So let me show you how Dynamics 365 Customer Service addresses each of these — starting with what happens when that phone rings..."

Tip
Read the room: If they're nodding enthusiastically, you've nailed their pain. If they seem lukewarm, ask "What did I miss?" — you may uncover a pain point that matters more to them. Don't dwell too long — acknowledge their challenges, then pivot to showing the solution.
