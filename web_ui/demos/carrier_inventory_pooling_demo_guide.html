1. Get your head locked in (5 min read)

The use case in one sentence

Carrier's aftermarket parts ordering process places POs with external suppliers (30–120 day lead times) even when sister Carrier factories already have the parts on hand — because KetteQ's MRP only looks at the aftermarket plant in SAP. We're flipping that to proactive daily flagging of pooling opportunities — with multi-source reasoning, not blanket rules.

2. The reframe — what to push back on

In the March 13 discovery call, Bobby focused on the bottom-left of the value stream map (3 deterministic business rules — PR Line Value > $6k, OH > Daily Usage × Lead Time, Lead Time > 60 days) and concluded:

"It's very deterministic rule based... it doesn't really fall on an agentic side... we need to make sure to align you with the right teams on our Microsoft side." — Bobby, ~34:00

That misclassified the problem. Joshua's actual reasoning narrative (~22:40–24:30) describes multi-source reasoning under uncertainty:

"...we can also see their inbound supply...we can assess to see if the inventory is in excess of what they need according to the demand in their plant...maybe they have a production order that needs one in a month from now, but they have a PO coming in next week...the better we can get at predicting [true availability], you know."

What to say if it comes up

"Great prep on this team's part — we took the value stream map back, built a working reasoning agent against mocked data, and we want to walk you through what the multi-source decisioning actually looks like before we declare what kind of solution this is. The deterministic rules are one input. The reasoning is what makes the planner trust it."

Don't argue. Demo. The four scenarios will make the point on their own.

3. What we actually built (last 24 hours)

Live, runnable, against mocked SAP/KetteQ data:

  • Discovery summary — transcript distilled into reasoning rubric + open questions
  • Architecture doc — component diagram, data contracts, roadmap
  • Mock dataset — 6 hand-crafted PRs across 6 reasoning scenarios + 3 plant snapshots (Charlotte, CMX-F, CMX-B)
  • Recommender agent — Python, computes signals deterministically, generates ranked recommendations + human-readable rationale, runs end-to-end
  • This demo guide

What the agent does

For each PR in the daily KetteQ batch:

  1. Pulls inventory snapshots for each candidate donor plant
  2. Computes true availability = on-hand − committed-production-through-needed-by + (inbound × supplier OTD%)
  3. Scores donor-vs-supplier-PO with confidence (max 95%) — derated when inbound dependence is high & supplier OTD is shaky
  4. Generates planner-facing rationale + suggested outreach text
  5. Ranks candidates by confidence × historical-say-yes-rate

What we deferred (intentionally)

  • Live SAP RP connector (Phase 2 plumbing — Bobby's demo already validated the path)
  • Dataverse persistence (Phase 2)
  • Power App / Code App planner inbox (Phase 2 — reuse trailer-prioritization scaffold)
  • Embedded Copilot Studio "ask why" (Phase 2)
  • Auto-STO creation (Phase 3 — gated on planner trust)

7. Talking-point cheat sheet

Phrases that work (anchor on Joshua's own words)

  • "Surface the conversation, don't replace it"
  • "Recommender, not transactor — Phase 1"
  • "True availability under uncertainty"
  • "Just as good at saying NO as YES"
  • "Trust is the entire battle"
  • "Volume metric is opportunity surfaced, not opportunity executed"

Phrases to avoid

  • ❌ "Automation" (sounds rule-based and steals their humans' jobs)
  • ❌ "AI will decide" (Joshua explicitly does NOT want this — donor plant must consent)
  • ❌ "We can do this in 2 weeks" (don't over-promise SAP integration)
  • ❌ "Bobby was wrong" (he just hadn't fully heard Joshua's reasoning narrative — frame it as "now that we've built it, here's what the agent actually does")

If they ask "why isn't this just a Power BI report?"

"A report would tell you 'CMX-F has 50.' The agent tells you 'CMX-F has 50, only 5 are committed to production through your needed-by date, their inbound from VND-1907 is risky, but they say yes 78% of the time and respond in 2 days.' That's the difference between a query and an agent."
