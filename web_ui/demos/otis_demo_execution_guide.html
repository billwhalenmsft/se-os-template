📖 The Demo Story — A Day at Otis EMEA Customer Care

Setting: You are Sarah, a Customer Service Representative at Otis EMEA's Manchester contact center. It's a busy Thursday morning. You've just logged into your new Dynamics 365 Customer Service workspace — Otis is piloting it to replace the legacy Ring Central system.

The Call: Your phone rings. The screen instantly shows caller information — it's David Chen, Facilities Manager at Riverside Medical Centre NHS Trust in London. Before you even say hello, you see: this is a Gold service contract customer, they have 8 elevators under contract, and there's an open case about a recent inspection.

The Problem: David reports a passenger elevator stuck between floors. Someone is trapped. You need to dispatch an engineer immediately while keeping David informed and calm.

The Differentiator: Unlike Ring Central, everything David needs is at your fingertips — no switching apps, no searching for records, no asking "Can you spell your name?" The system knows who's calling.

↗️ Warm Transfer & Collaboration

Hand off with context—no cold transfers
~5 min
▼
Scenario: Escalate to Engineering
Say

David's situation is urgent—someone's trapped. Sarah needs to bring in the engineering dispatch team. With Ring Central, she'd transfer the call and David would have to explain everything again. Let's see how D365 handles this.

Click
In the call controls, click Transfer → Consult (warm transfer).
Show
The transfer dialog shows:
  • Queue selector: "Engineering Dispatch"
  • Available agents in that queue
  • Option to add notes before transfer
Say

Sarah can do a warm transfer—she stays on the line, briefs the engineer, then drops off. Or she can add a note that travels with the transfer. Either way, the engineer sees everything: David's record, the account, the case, the whole timeline. No more 'Can you start from the beginning?'

🎯 DEMO GOLD: "This is what 35 agents × 50 calls/day looks like at scale. Every transfer keeps context. Every escalation preserves history. Your customers don't repeat themselves. Your agents don't waste time."
