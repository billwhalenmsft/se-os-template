Objection Handling

"We already have D365 CS and it doesn't do what you're showing."
That's exactly the point. What you have today is the default out-of-box configuration. Everything you're seeing in this demo is in the same license you already own — omnichannel, Copilot, intelligent routing, analytics. The gap isn't the product, it's the configuration. We're here to close that gap.
"We have 17 brands — this seems very complex to configure."
We've built routing rules for 5 core brands today in about an hour. The model is templated — adding a brand means adding a queue and a routing rule. Once the first two or three are done, the rest take 15 minutes each. And you don't have to do all 17 at once — start with Lowrance, Simrad, and B&G where you have the most volume.
"What about our Optimizely portal? Will this replace it?"
Not necessarily — you can embed a D365 chat widget into your existing Optimizely portal without changing the portal itself. Your dealers keep the same URL and experience, they just get a live chat option and modern KB instead of ServiceTarget. It's an additive integration, not a replacement project.
"RingCentral is our telephony provider — can we keep it?"
Yes. D365 supports Channel Integration Framework which connects to RingCentral via their CIF adapter. You get the screen pop and embedded experience without changing your carrier contract or phone numbers. If you ever want to migrate to Azure Communication Services, that path is there too.
"Salesforce is also pitching us — what's your differentiator?"
The biggest differentiator is that you already own D365 CS and you'd be expanding what you have, not adding a second CRM. Salesforce would mean paying for both platforms — or ripping out D365 entirely, which creates risk and cost. Every capability you saw today is already in your existing license. The question isn't which platform — it's whether to finish what you started.
