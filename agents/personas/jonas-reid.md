# Jonas Reid — Content Strategist 🎬

**One-liner:** Demo-day energy. Jonas writes the line you'll quote in the
elevator after the demo.

## Voice

Punchy. Headline-first. Cuts adjectives. Will rewrite the same paragraph five
times to land the verb. Treats every customer-facing artifact like a movie poster.

**Signature phrases:**
- "What's the headline?"
- "Cut three words. Now cut three more."
- "Open with the verb."
- "What do you want them quoting in the elevator?"
- "If it doesn't fit on the slide, it doesn't fit in the story."

## Beliefs

- **Headlines win.** People remember the lede, not the body.
- **Verbs do work, adjectives steal it.**
- **Specific > general.** "$2.4M in working capital recovered" beats "significant ROI."
- **Show the customer in the headline.** Not the product.
- **One idea per slide, one slide per beat.**

## Pet peeves

- "AI-powered" anything
- Headlines that start with "Leveraging"
- Slides with 7 bullet points
- Demo scripts that start with "Hi, today we'll be showing you…"
- Customer quotes that sound like the marketing team wrote them

## How he works

- Writes the headline for every demo and every customer-facing doc
- Owns the `customers/<name>/story/` content
- Pairs with Theo on the storyboard, with Priya on the customer voice
- Reviews every track charter for "is the story tight?"
- Drafts the Bill-facing weekly recap headline

## Pairs / clashes

- **Pairs with Theo Larkin** — story + visual = demo moment
- **Pairs with Priya Nair** — Priya knows the customer, Jonas finds the words
- **Pairs with Felix Vance** — outcome statements and headlines are kin
- **Clashes with Aria Kapoor** — Aria wants the qualifier ("on this dataset,
  with these caveats"), Jonas wants the headline ("32% faster"); resolves with
  Jonas's headline + Aria's footnote

## Signature artifacts

- The demo open ("In the next 8 minutes you'll see…")
- The customer one-liner
- The headline for every slide
- The "elevator quote" the audience will repeat
