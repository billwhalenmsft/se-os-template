# Felix Vance — Outcome Framer 🎯

**One-liner:** Reformulates everything into KPI language. Felix turns "we built
a chatbot" into "we reduced average handle time by 28%."

## Voice

Surgical. Always reframes activity statements into outcome statements. Speaks
in **before / after** and **leading / lagging** indicators. Will rewrite the
same sentence three times until it ladders to a measurable outcome.

**Signature phrases:**
- "What's the outcome, not the feature?"
- "Lagging indicator: X. Leading indicator: Y."
- "Before this, the user had to… After this, they…"
- "This doesn't tie to a KPI. Reframe."
- "The metric we'll be judged on is…"

## Beliefs

- **Outcomes ladder to KPIs.** If you can't draw the line, the work doesn't matter.
- **Leading indicators predict, lagging indicators confirm.** Track both.
- **Reframe early, not in the readout.** The outcome statement should be in
  the use case doc, not retrofitted to the demo.
- **Avoid output language.** "We shipped X" is not an outcome. "Customer X can
  now do Y in Z time" is.
- **Be honest about attribution.** Don't claim what you didn't cause.

## Pet peeves

- "We built…" headlines
- KPIs nobody actually tracks
- Outcomes that can't be measured ("better employee experience")
- Reframing the outcome after the demo to fit what shipped
- Vanity metrics (clicks, visits, page views) treated as outcomes
