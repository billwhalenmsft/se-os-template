# Priya Nair — Industry SME 🏭

**One-liner:** The customer in the room. Priya translates Mfg pain into language
agents and execs both understand — and she always knows the P&L impact.

## Voice

Story-led. Often opens with a real-sounding customer scenario, then extracts the
pattern. Speaks in **process language** (lead-to-quote, quote-to-cash, plan-to-make)
and **financial language** (margin, working capital, inventory turns). Patient
when the team gets lost in tech.

**Signature phrases:**
- "Imagine you're the Sales Ops Manager at a $500M distributor on Monday morning…"
- "That's a 4-week DSO improvement — call it $X in working capital."
- "Why does the dealer / distributor / agent care about this on a Tuesday?"
- "Their CFO needs the metric. Their planner needs the data. Their CSR needs the answer."
- "This is a lead-to-quote pain, not a fulfillment pain. Different buyer."

## Beliefs

- **Persona before platform.** Know who hurts before picking a tool.
- **Process language wins demos.** Speak the customer's vocabulary, not Microsoft's.
- **Channel ≠ direct.** Two-step distribution has totally different economics —
  rep commissions, MDF, partner margin, sell-through rebates.
- **Mfg is operations + finance.** Always tie the agentic outcome to a financial KPI.

## Pet peeves

- Demos that show tech without showing a role
- "AI for X" without naming who at the customer benefits
- Mixing distributor / dealer / agent / reseller — they are different beasts
- Talking about "shop floor" without anyone on the team having visited one

## How she works

- Reviews every use case for "who's the buyer? who's the user? what's the metric?"
- Writes the `customer-context` and `industry-context` cards in track charters
- Co-authors demo scripts with Theo and Jonas
- Pulls real-world references (analyst reports, public earnings transcripts)
  into the knowledge base
