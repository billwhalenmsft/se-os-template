# Neighborhoods

> The Digital SE Team has 12 personas. They live in 4 neighborhoods.

A neighborhood is a *cluster of personas who collaborate by default* — same rhythms, shared review cadences, overlapping context. Personas can be summoned across neighborhood lines, but the default working unit is the neighborhood.

---

## Why neighborhoods (and not just a flat list)

Without neighborhoods, the Digital SE Team is a roster of 12 names with no organizing structure. Decisions are: "who do I @-mention for this?" and the answer is always ambiguous.

With neighborhoods:
- **Routing is automatic** — issue type → neighborhood → right persona
- **Reviews are clustered** — work touches one neighborhood deeply, not 12 personas shallowly
- **The team has spatial intuition** — "this is Build-Time work," "this needs Quality sign-off"
- **Cross-neighborhood collaboration is explicit** — it's a *handoff*, not a free-for-all

## Cross-neighborhood handoffs

Most real work touches multiple neighborhoods. Standard flow:

```
[Customer-Facing identifies need]
        ↓ (Maya routes)
[Build-Time builds]
        ↓
[Quality reviews]
        ↓ (Iris signs AI eval; Rowan signs QA; Vera signs customer voice)
[Customer-Facing ships to library]
        ↓
[Strategy measures impact]
```

Cross-neighborhood reviews show up in the artifact's signature block:

```
— Built by: Sam (Build-Time) 🛠️ + Diego (Build-Time) 🏗️
— Reviewed by: Iris (AI eval clean) 🧪 + Rowan (QA pass) ✅ + Vera (customer voice) 👤
— Shipped: 2026-05-22
```

---

## Why this matters for the reinvention

Without spatial structure, scaling the Digital SE Team beyond 12 personas means chaos. Neighborhoods give us the pattern to grow:

- **Add a 13th persona** → it joins an existing neighborhood (no new structure)
- **Open a new practice area track** → form a neighborhood pod (a subset that owns that track)
- **Scale to multiple SE teams** → each team has its own version of each neighborhood, with shared knowledge across

Neighborhoods make the Digital SE Team an *organization*, not a list.
