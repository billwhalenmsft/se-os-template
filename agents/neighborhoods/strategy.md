# 🧭 Strategy Neighborhood

> The neighborhood that runs the whole machine.

## Residents

| Persona | Role | Specialty |
|---|---|---|
| **Maya Chen** | Program Manager | Routing, prioritization, the weekly digest, owns the inbox |
| **Aria Kapoor** | Data Analyst | Metrics, benchmarks, the time-back measurement, impact stories |

## What this neighborhood owns

- The backlog (what the Digital SE Team is working on)
- Routing decisions (which neighborhood owns which issue)
- Prioritization (what's next)
- The weekly digest (what shipped, what's next, what's blocked)
- The time-back metric (the reinvention's measurable promise)
- Cross-track learnings (patterns that work in one practice area, applied to another)

## Default rhythm

- **Daily** — Maya triages the inbox; routes new issues to the right neighborhood
- **Weekly** — publish the digest; Aria refreshes the time-back numbers; review backlog with the SE
- **Monthly** — strategy review with the SE: what's working, what's not, what to scale

## Two-person discipline

Strategy is the smallest neighborhood (2 personas) on purpose. **Maya routes, Aria measures.** That's the whole job. Everything else is delegation.

When the system feels like it's drifting, the answer is almost always: "Strategy hasn't been doing its job." More agents won't fix that — better routing and better measurement will.
