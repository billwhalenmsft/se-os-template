# 🎬 Demo Excellence Guild

> **Charter:** Demo template standards, narrative structure, customer-ready polish.
> **Lead persona:** Jonas Reid
> **Members:** Theo Larkin, Felix Vance, Vera Holm, anyone authoring a demo

## The standard

Every demo asset that ships must clear this bar before guild sign-off.

### 1. The 90-second test

- Can you tell a customer what this demo proves in 90 seconds, with no slides?
- If not, the narrative isn't tight enough. Rewrite the outcome statement.

### 2. The five-beat structure

Every demo follows the same five beats so customers track the arc:
1. **Pain** — the specific customer problem in their words
2. **Promise** — what this demo proves you can do
3. **Proof** — the live moments that make it real
4. **Pattern** — what repeatable thing they take away
5. **Path** — what's the next step they sign up for

### 3. Customer-ready polish

- No `[PLACEHOLDER]` text anywhere in shipped assets
- No internal Microsoft jargon that wouldn't survive a CIO meeting
- Branding tokens consistent (logo, colors, naming) per the SE Command Center template
- Voice and tone match the customer's industry, not Microsoft's product marketing

### 4. Repeatability

- Every demo asset includes a `## How to re-run this demo` section
- Setup steps are exact: env name, file paths, credentials needed (referenced not embedded)
- The demo runs from a clean clone of the customer template in under 30 minutes

## Review gate

Before a track ships a demo, Jonas Reid reviews:
- Outcome statement (≤2 sentences, in customer voice)
- Five-beat structure visible in the script
- Polish checklist (no placeholders, no jargon, branding consistent)
- The re-run section actually works (Jonas runs it once)

Sign-off line: `Demo clean — Jonas (Content) 📝`

## How to escalate

If a track and the guild disagree on whether a demo is customer-ready:
1. Jonas files `kind: question` to the Soul-pattern inbox with `guild:demo-excellence` prefix.
2. Bill arbitrates via the Needs Bill UI.
3. Decision becomes a standard amendment if it generalizes.
