# 🤝 Customer Empathy Guild

> **Charter:** Outcome framing, persona accuracy, "would a real customer care?" gate.
> **Lead persona:** Vera Holm
> **Members:** Felix Vance, Priya Nair, Jonas Reid, anyone writing customer-facing artifacts

## The standard

Every customer-facing artifact must clear this bar before guild sign-off.

### 1. The "would they care?" test

- Read the artifact as if you're the customer's primary persona for it.
- If the first paragraph doesn't make that persona say "yes, that's me / yes, I want that,"
  the framing is wrong.
- Rewrite from the persona's job-to-be-done, not from Microsoft's product capabilities.

### 2. Persona accuracy

- Personas are not job titles. They have specific pains, words, and metrics.
- Use customer language (not Microsoft language) for what they do and care about.
- When in doubt, pull a real quote from a customer call into the persona card.

### 3. Outcome framing

- Every artifact opens with an **outcome statement** in this exact form:
  > "For [persona] at [company type], who needs to [job], this gives them [outcome]
  > so they can [downstream value]."
- If you can't write that sentence in customer voice, you don't yet understand the use case.

### 4. The "no PowerPoint" sniff test

- Could a customer use this artifact without anyone from Microsoft narrating it?
- If not, what's missing? Usually: the persona context, the outcome upfront, or the next step.

## Review gate

Before a track ships a customer-facing artifact, Vera Holm reviews:
- Outcome statement matches the customer persona's actual words
- Persona card reflects a real customer (not a stereotype)
- The artifact stands on its own without a Microsoft narrator
- Next step is concrete and customer-driven (not "let us know if interested")

Sign-off line: `Customer-ready — Vera (Customer Persona) 👤`

## How to escalate

If a track and the guild disagree on whether an artifact reflects the customer:
1. Vera files `kind: question` to the Soul-pattern inbox with `guild:customer-empathy` prefix.
2. Bill arbitrates via the Needs Bill UI (he's the closest to real customers).
3. Decision becomes a standard amendment if it generalizes.
