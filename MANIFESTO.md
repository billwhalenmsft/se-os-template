# The SE Reinvention Manifesto

> A statement of what it means to be a Microsoft Sales Engineer in the AI era.

---

## We believe

**The Sales Engineer role has not kept up with what AI now makes possible.**

Today's SE spends 60% of their time on prep, context-assembly, and asset rebuilding — work that AI can now do. The other 40% is where the actual value is: deep technical conversation with customers, architecting solutions, and judgment.

We're going to flip those numbers.

---

## The Five Convictions

### 1. SEs are architects, not assemblers.
Stop hand-building what an agent can build. The SE's unique value is **judgment, strategy, and customer empathy** — not stitching together 7 tabs of context before every meeting. Every minute spent assembling is a minute not spent thinking.

### 2. Knowledge must compound.
Today, each SE rebuilds the same demo assets from scratch. Each customer conversation generates insight that dies in someone's OneNote. **In SE OS, every interaction makes the system smarter for the next SE.** Knowledge is the team's most undervalued asset. We're fixing that.

### 3. Agents are colleagues, not chatbots.
The Digital SE Team has names, voices, opinions, and accountability. **They sign their work. They push back when something is wrong.** They are not a productivity tool — they are 12 teammates who never sleep and never forget.

### 4. The customer time is the only time that matters.
Every SE OS feature is judged by one metric: **does it return time to the customer conversation?** Internal dashboards, agent UIs, and tooling are all means to that end. We measure what we mean to do.

### 5. Quality compounds — or trust collapses.
Speed without quality is debt. The three guilds (AI Craft, Demo Excellence, Customer Empathy) exist because **a single bad customer-facing artifact poisons trust faster than a hundred good ones build it**. The bar is non-negotiable.

## What changes

### For the individual SE

| Before SE OS | After SE OS |
|---|---|
| 2–3 hours of prep before every customer meeting | 15 minutes with the Command Center |
| Demo assets live in personal OneDrive folders | Shared library, auto-tailored to each customer |
| New SE ramps over 6+ months | New SE productive within a week |
| "I'll get back to you on that" (because the data is in 5 places) | "Here's the answer" (because the agents already have the context) |
| Best practice = what worked last time for *me* | Best practice = what worked across the *team* |

### For the SE team

- Onboarding new SEs goes from quarters to days
- Practice areas get *real* documentation that stays current
- Demo libraries grow without anyone explicitly tending them
- SE leadership gets actual data on where SE time is spent

### For the customer

- Faster, more tailored responses
- SEs show up better-prepared, more relevant, more focused
- Less "let me check on that" and more "yes, and here's how"

---

## What this is not

This is **not** about replacing SEs with AI. The opposite — it's about **amplifying** SEs by removing the work that doesn't require human judgment.

This is **not** another productivity tool. It is the operating system that integrates the productivity tools an SE already uses (Teams, Outlook, M365 Copilot, D365, Power Platform, Azure, GitHub, CDX) into one coherent surface.

This is **not** a research project. It exists today, it deploys today, and Microsoft SEs can use it today.

When an SE adopts SE OS, within 60 days:

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
